About me

Hi, my name is Rachael and I've been in the fitness industry since 2006. I first started playing with basic yoga poses at a very young age, watching my father and copying his movements but it was when going through a spell of depression in my late teens that I turned to yoga, exercise and a healthy diet, primarily as something positive to focus on. The change was astonishing! I went from having no energy and low self-esteem to having bundles of energy and finally being able to start appreciating myself. Having felt the emotional changes and seen the physical changes, I started studying anatomy, yoga, exercise formats and nutrition as a hobby. At 22 I flew off to Mexico to work as part of a sports and entertainment team in a hotel on the coast for 2 years, giving swimming lessons, aerobics, and stretching classes. 

I followed this with training to be a personal trainer and yoga teacher and began working in Nuffield Health Gym in Cambridge. During the 4 years I worked there I expanded my repertoire and certified as a Thai Massage therapist. Changing careers to work in something I'm passionate about, is an integral part of my everyday life and is constantly teaching me new and exciting things, has been a huge blessing. I feel honoured to be on this journey and to be able to share what I am discovering with others.

What I have learned and am still learning is that exercise is not only an instrument for physical change but also a tool to strengthen the mind and to reduce stress and anxiety.

Yoga

Yoga Styles

Power yoga

Power Yoga finds it's roots in Ashtanga and Vinyasa Yoga. A typical class includes a good number of vinyasas or sun salutations. It strengthens and tones the body through the use of a person's bodyweight in various different dynamic and isometric poses and exercises.

Vinyasa Flow Yoga:

Similar to power yoga yet gentler. Vinyasa is the marriage of movement and breath. It is a dynamic practice that teaches you to meditate as you move and that allows the body to build up heat progressively through the class, allowing for deep stretches that rapidly improve flexibility and mobility.

Soft Yoga

A therapeutic practice, soft yoga helps to calm the mind and stretch the body. The classes are varied and focus on a mixture of gentle stretches, back strengthening exercises and mobilizing movements.

Hatha Yoga

The term Hatha Yoga encompasses any, or most, of the physical practices of yoga and therefore can be a very varied style. This means the classes can be adapted to the practitioner's needs.

Yin Yoga

A gentle physical practice, Yin Yoga helps to calm the mind, lower the heart rate and helps the body to enter into a peaceful state. Poses are held for a minimum of 3 minutes, allowing time to focus on breath and to enter into a meditative state.
